The problem

Most prospecting tools give agencies too many weak leads.

Generic lead lists are full of companies with no clear SEO gap, no reachable contact path, no budget signal, or no reason to pitch SEO.

1

Too much noise

Large lists look impressive, but agencies waste time sorting through low-quality prospects.

2

No pitch reason

A company name and phone number are not enough. Your team needs to know why SEO is relevant.

3

Missing contacts

If there is no phone, email, or usable contact path, the record is not a real sales opportunity.

The RankForge method

Every business is scored before it becomes a lead.

RankForge looks for the combination that matters: SEO need, commercial fit, contact readiness, and useful context for agency outreach.

SEO Need

Weak local SEO, thin service pages, poor metadata, missing location coverage, and crawl quality issues.

Commercial Fit

Local service categories, high customer value, paid ads signals, and buyer-intent website language.

Contact Confidence

Phone, email, contact page, homepage/footer data, and source evidence are checked before display.

Lead Priority

Qualified and review-needed records are organized into saved lists that agencies can inspect and export.
