Example Work

Workflow automation examples built around real operations.

These examples show the kind of work Crestline Ops builds: focused systems that take a painful manual process and turn it into a cleaner flow with records, review points, alerts, and logs.

Example system 01

Document intake to clean records.

PDFsSpreadsheetsReview queue

Files arrive, the important fields are extracted, and uncertain values are flagged.

Useful for teams copying data from property files, applications, reports, contracts, invoices, or recurring spreadsheets into Sheets or a CRM.

InputEmail attachments, Drive folders, forms, and uploaded files.
LogicExtract fields, normalize values, check required columns, detect missing data, and flag low-confidence results.
OutputClean rows, review status, run logs, alerts, and CRM-ready records.

Example system 02

Lead research and reply tracking.

ResearchQualificationInbox alerts

Prospects are researched, grouped by fit, and kept visible after outreach.

Useful for teams that want a careful lead system without turning outreach into chaotic spreadsheet work.

InputTarget market, company type, source lists, websites, and contact paths.
LogicScore fit, check do-not-contact rules, prepare drafts, control sending, and classify replies.
OutputQualified leads, draft queues, send logs, reply alerts, and follow-up statuses.

Example system 03

CRM update and task routing workflow.

CRM automationTask routingPipeline visibility

Records stay cleaner because the next action is created from the workflow itself.

Useful for teams that lose time updating contacts, deals, follow-ups, notes, and owner assignments after forms, emails, documents, or lead sources arrive.

InputForms, email inquiries, spreadsheet rows, CRM changes, and new lead sources.
LogicCreate records, update fields, assign owners, flag duplicates, and open review tasks when the data is incomplete.
OutputCleaner CRM records, visible next actions, reply alerts, task queues, and weekly exception summaries.

What every build needs

The system should be useful even when something goes wrong.

Clear logs

Runs should show what happened, what was skipped, and what needs review.

Review points

Uncertain fields, risky actions, and exceptions should pause for a person.

Simple handoff

The output should land where the team already works, not in another place to babysit.
